FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are StackBid estimates? +

Our estimates are based on current wholesale market data and are meant to be a close reference point for actual supplier quotes. Prices vary by region, supplier, and season. We always recommend getting at least 2-3 quotes from local suppliers before purchasing.

What is the difference between the three price columns? +

HD / Lowe's Retail — standard walk-in shelf price at big box stores. Generally the most expensive option.

Wholesale Estimate — typical contractor/trade pricing, generally below retail. Available to contractors and some homeowners with accounts.

Local Supplier — price from the nearest local building materials supplier in your ZIP code area. Can be a strong option for larger projects.

Is StackBid free to use? +

Your first estimate is completely free — just your email, no credit card. After that, StackBid Pro ($9.99/month) gives you unlimited estimates, saved PDFs, and local supplier and contractor options.

How does billing work? +

StackBid Pro is $9.99/month, billed automatically every month starting the day you subscribe. Your card is charged through Stripe. We'll email you a confirmation right after you subscribe, and there are no long-term contracts — you're billed month to month.

How do I cancel my subscription? +

You can cancel anytime from your account page — click "Manage / Cancel Subscription" to open your billing portal and cancel in a couple of clicks. No phone call or email needed. You'll keep Pro access until the end of your current billing period, and you won't be charged again.

Does the estimate include labor costs? +

Yes — alongside your materials breakdown, StackBid now shows an estimated labor cost range based on regional wage data and typical hours for your project type, plus a combined Total Project Cost. Actual labor cost varies by contractor, complexity, and local demand, so we recommend getting 2-3 local quotes to confirm.

Are the contractors listed on StackBid verified? +

Contractors in our database have provided license numbers. However, we strongly recommend independently verifying any contractor's license through your state licensing board before hiring. StackBid does not endorse specific contractors.

How do I get the best price on materials? +

Use your StackBid estimate as a baseline, then: (1) Contact your local supplier shown in the third column, (2) Ask about bulk discounts for large orders, (3) Consider buying during off-season when prices are lower, (4) Ask your contractor if they can use their trade account for better pricing.

I am a contractor. How do I get listed on StackBid? +

Sign up directly on the site for a 30-day free Pro trial ($49/month after) — priority placement and lead notifications start immediately. A verified badge is added once StackBid confirms your license. Questions? hello@stackbid.app
