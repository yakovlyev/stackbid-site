FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are StackBid estimates? +

Our estimates are based on 2026 wholesale market data and are typically within 10-15% of actual supplier quotes. Prices vary by region, supplier, and season. We always recommend getting at least 2-3 quotes from local suppliers before purchasing.

What is the difference between the three price columns? +

HD / Lowe's Retail — standard walk-in shelf price at big box stores. The most expensive option.

Wholesale Estimate — typical contractor/trade pricing, approximately 25% below retail. Available to contractors and some homeowners with accounts.

Local Supplier — price from the nearest local building materials supplier in your ZIP code area. Often the best value for larger projects.

Is StackBid free to use? +

Yes, StackBid is completely free for homeowners. You can generate unlimited estimates, save PDFs, and access local supplier and contractor information at no cost.

Does the estimate include labor costs? +

No — StackBid estimates cover materials only. Labor costs vary widely by contractor, region, and project complexity. Use our contractor cards to contact local professionals for labor quotes.

Are the contractors listed on StackBid verified? +

Contractors in our database have provided license numbers. However, we strongly recommend independently verifying any contractor's license through your state licensing board before hiring. StackBid does not endorse specific contractors.

How do I get the best price on materials? +

Use your StackBid estimate as a baseline, then: (1) Contact your local supplier shown in the third column, (2) Ask about bulk discounts for large orders, (3) Consider buying during off-season when prices are lower, (4) Ask your contractor if they can use their trade account for better pricing.
